The Various Disciplines of Rock Climbing

By: Theodore Yochum

Introduction

Rock Climbing is a sport that, like many others has changed and developed drastically over the past decades. As an activity its roots are found in the human mind as no one needed to invent it, humans are just drawn to climbing things out of necessity or for fun. Generally climbing will refer to a person using their arms and legs to climb a rock face that is too steep to do with just your feet. When people refer to climbing a mountain, this will usually refer to a form of hiking or backpacking instead of what is considered climbing as most climbing occurs on a single face or system of rock, which in certain cases may be an entire mountain but, usually is only anywhere from 50 to 200 or so feet high. However, climbing as a sport does have a few disciplines that will be covered below.
